Why I like research?

I like research because it satisfies my curiosity and drive to understand the world more deeply. The process of exploring new ideas, asking questions, and finding answers through investigation is incredibly rewarding. Research allows me to challenge existing knowledge, think critically, and contribute to solving real-world problems. It's like being a detective, uncovering insights that others might overlook, and the journey from discovery to application feels meaningful. I enjoy the sense of accomplishment that comes from finding connections, making new contributions to a field, and continuously learning in the process.

In my free time

I always go outside to reseach.

Belive me!

Going out to research is an exciting and immersive experience that brings a different dimension to learning. Unlike desk-bound research, field research allows you to directly engage with the subject matter in its natural environment. Whether you’re collecting data, observing behaviors, conducting interviews, or exploring new locations, being out in the field provides real-world context to your findings. Field research can also lead to unexpected discoveries. By stepping outside, you can encounter variables and experiences that are often missed in controlled environments. The dynamic and unpredictable nature of fieldwork encourages adaptability and sharpens your observational skills. Additionally, going out to research connects you with people and places, offering new perspectives and enriching your understanding. The interactions and hands-on experiences make the research more tangible, meaningful, and impactful. Ultimately, it adds a sense of adventure and purpose to your work, reminding you that learning isn’t just confined to books and screens—it's all around you, waiting to be explored.
